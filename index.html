About Me:

I was born in Jamaica to an indian family. Starting my PhD journey in Physics at CUNY where my research interest lie heavily on large simulations of the cosmic web and quenching phenomena along filaments plunging into galaxy clusters. Outside of that I love to paint,draw, printmake, read, listen to music, try to play some moosic,find old things,cook some weird stuff and do some fun stuff in the GREAT outdoors.

Most recent random thought: You can do almost anything on the New York subway.
Research Updates: Just the start of my work with Professor Welker over at City Tech! Starting to get familiar with running jobs on a cluster. So far, we have been making headway in comparing the difference in filament structures due to the resolution of the simulation one uses. Moving forward we are hoping to study the effects of magnetic draping on larger scales namely, around halo structures.
